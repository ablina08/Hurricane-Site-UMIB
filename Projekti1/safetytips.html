Be Prepared for Hurricanes

Essential tips and guidelines to help you stay safe before, during, and after a hurricane.

Hurricane Safety Tips

1. Know Your Risks

Identify if you live in a hurricane-prone area and understand the possible hazards in your region, such as storm surge or flooding.

2. Create a Plan

Develop an emergency plan with evacuation routes, designated safe areas, and a communication plan for your family.

3. Prepare an Emergency Kit

Gather essential supplies such as water, non-perishable food, medications, a flashlight, batteries, and first-aid kits.

4. Secure Your Home

Ensure your home is well-prepared by installing shutters, securing doors and windows, and removing outdoor items that could become projectiles.

5. Stay Informed

Monitor weather updates and alerts from trusted sources. Stay connected with local authorities and emergency services.

6. Evacuate if Necessary

If authorities issue an evacuation order, leave early and follow the recommended evacuation routes to avoid getting stuck in traffic.

7. Protect Your Vehicle

Move your vehicle to higher ground if possible and ensure it's fueled in case evacuation is necessary.

8. Prepare Your Pets

Ensure that you have enough food, water, and medications for your pets. Have a plan for evacuating them as well.

9. Check Insurance Coverage

Review your insurance policy to ensure you're covered for hurricane-related damages, especially flooding.

10. Avoid Flooded Areas

Avoid walking or driving through flooded areas. Even shallow water can sweep you away or damage your vehicle.

11. Stock up on Gas

Make sure your vehicle and any portable generators are filled with gas. Gas stations may be closed or have long lines during and after the storm.

12. Strengthen Your Roof

Consider reinforcing your roof if you live in an area with frequent hurricanes. Use hurricane straps to secure the roof to the walls and foundation.
